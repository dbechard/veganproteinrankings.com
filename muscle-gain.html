Best Vegan Protein Powder for Muscle Gain

Muscle-bound vegans aren’t urban legends. They’re lifters who pick powders that pull their weight. Building new fiber means hitting about 1.6–2.2 g protein per kg of body weight daily, and every scoop needs a solid shot of leucine (think 2.5 g or more) so mTOR flips from nap to grow. A 2023 systematic review found plant proteins deliver muscle gains comparable to animal sources when total protein is matched, so your biceps don’t care whether their bricks came from peas or pastures.*

Pea protein gets special billing because its leucine hit rivals whey. In an eight-week study on novice lifters, pea supplementation added as much muscle thickness to the elbow flexors as whey and out-grew placebo—clear proof you can curl legumes for size.*

Rice isn’t just for sushi rolls. Researchers giving 48 g of rice protein isolate post-workout saw the same strength and body-comp gains as an equal whey dose after eight weeks of hardcore lifting, suggesting grain-based powder can patch torn fibers just as fast.*

If you worry plant blends lag in essential amino acids, newer evidence disagrees. A 2024 narrative review reported plant mixes fortified with BCAAs—or simply dosed higher—stimulate muscle protein synthesis on par with milk and beef proteins. Translation: bump the serving or grab a blend spiked with extra leucine and the growth signal rings loud.*
